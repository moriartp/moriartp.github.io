Federal Employee Viewpoint Survey



In 2015, more than 400,000 employees participated in the Federal Employee Viewpoint Survey (FEVS). Employee feedback on key performance metrics captured in the FEVS helps to realizing the federal Office of Personell Management's mission to maintain and enhance a talented, engaged, and diverse Federal workforce. The feedback provided enables each agency to develop effective strategies and tools for driving continuous improvement. On this site you will find two tools for use in analyzing the results of this survey.

Summary
Overview of the 2015 survey: All survey statement/questions are grouped by section. Sections descend in clockwise fashion depicting the highest to lowest average rate of positive response. Within each section, the individual questions are also sorted by this manner. Further, color scaling indicates differences in positive response rates from that the overall survey average, highlighting the greatest deviations from the average.

Agency Rating
All agencies are accountable for ensuring that the Federal Government recruits, retains, and honors a world-class workforce - one that gets things done. These ratings reflect where agencies stand on meeting that expectation.The ratings are based on percent positive scores of the items that make up each index. The ratings provide a reliable assessment of where agencies fall within each index: HCAAF, Employee Engagement, Global Satisfaction, and the New IQ, and this visualization tool permits you to examin the relationship between them over time, beginning with the 2011 survey results.
