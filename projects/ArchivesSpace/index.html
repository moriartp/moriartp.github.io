ArchivesSpace Phase II

Sponsor(s): Jim Cheng, Sean Quimby, Carol Ann Fabian
Project Owner: Kevin Schlottman

Current library archivists’ workflows relating to records management of archival materials are variously executed and rely on a multitude of records processing systems and touchpoints. This has contributed to the current backlog of archival materials processing. Further, the variousness of workflow routines, touchpoint systems, and finding aid formats has contributed to a lack of standardization, records management capability, and quality control assurances.

This project we will aim to better utilize the newly implemented ArchivesSpace hosted platform to increase efficiency, standardization and quality assurance practices as it relates to management of records for the Libraries archival materials.

The goals for this project include various aims related to streamlining workflow processes, processing efficiencies and standards:

Goal Description Priority
Centralize/Consolidate Collection Management Systems Reduce system headcount in the processing workflow, simplify archives processing technology ecosystem front-end. High
Reduce Archives Backlog Streamline processes, gain operational insight, and build capacity. High
Improve Customer Services Reduce service lags/increase immediacy of patron access to archival materials. Low
Environmental Security Ensure the integrity and security of archival records. Medium

Empower Staff

Support individual agency and create structures that allow staff to act nimbly, provoke change, and assert our roles in substantive academic endeavor.
By providing improved tools and technological mechanisms to process archival accessions, this project will directly contribute the the processing capacity of library staff archivists.

Commit to Inclusion

Challenge the organization to enable a thriving, inclusive community by embedding support and welcome for all identities and cultural experiences in our efforts, conversations, and actions.
Implicit in the design and functional capability of the solution is flexible support mechanisms that accommodate the dynamic and varied range of materials held by the libraries. As such the solution allows us to serve the diverse needs for language and character rendering.

Catalyze Discovery

Cultivate a campus research environment that generates a wellspring of expertise, accelerates the production of new knowledge, and amplifies research outcomes.
By increasing our processing capacity this project will help to introduce an ever-broader range of materials into the university’s research environment, and therefore directly contributes to the production of new knowledge.

Advance Knowledge

Build, steward, and leverage collections distinguished by scope, quality, and scale to inform memory and enhance scholarship.
This project directly serves our aims to scale our collection to inform memory and enhance scholarship.

Processing Capacity
By centralizing a single source of truth for the processing of Archival materials, and streamlining the workflow necessary to process archival accessions, success for this project would be realized by an increase in the rate of processing for archival materials. This may be assessed by comparing processing volumes over a sixth month period both before and after the project deliverables are deployed to library operations.
